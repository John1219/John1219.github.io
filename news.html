Week 1 Start/Sit Angles Draft Prep

1) Season Openers & Primetime Highlights

  • Thursday Night Football Kickoff: The 2025 season opens with the reigning Super Bowl champion Philadelphia Eagles hosting the Dallas Cowboys at 8:20 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. Fantasy angle: Expect heavy usage for the Eagles’ core; A.J. Brown profiles as a top-end WR1 in kickoff game scripts.
  • Monday Night Football Special: Week 1 closes on Sept 8 with ESPN’s MNF showcase (Bears vs. Vikings), wall-to-wall coverage from afternoon through late night. Fantasy angle: Late-window roster pivots—keep an extra bench WR/RB for contingency swaps.

2) RedZone Changes

The commercial-free RedZone experience is giving way to ad breaks.
  • Viewer impact: Expect intermittent interruptions during witching hour.
  • Strategy tweak: Lean on alerts and box-score apps to avoid missing snap-to-snap usage spikes.

3) Media Moves & Broadcast Battles

  • Platform shuffle: ESPN expands its NFL footprint; shifts to streaming will continue to reshape where fantasy content lives.
  • Talent notes: Jason Kelce’s appearances are constrained by network exclusivity clauses—expect continued fragmentation across platforms.

Fantasy takeaway: Know where your pre-kickoff shows, injury hits, and film rooms air so you’re not scrambling on Sunday morning.

4) Coaching Shake-Ups & Strategic Storylines

  • Raiders’ new look: Chip Kelly joins as offensive coordinator under Pete Carroll. Fantasy angle: Early-season volatility—monitor pace and RB target share; stash boom-bust WRs with YAC upside.
  • Opening test: New-era Vegas draws a road game at the Patriots. Start/Sit: Fringe Raiders are matchup-dependent FLEX plays until roles stabilize.

5) Player Health & Returns to Watch

  • Christian McCaffrey: Trending toward RB1 usage if workload is full—prioritize handcuff depth.
  • Dak Prescott: Back in QB1 mix; stacks with Lamb/Cooks remain high-ceiling tournament plays.

6) Draft Intel & Week 1 Matchup Signals

  • Model leans: Analytics tilt toward the Eagles in the opener.
  • Prep flow: Expect last-minute rankings, shadow reports, and actives/inactives to swing FLEX decisions.
